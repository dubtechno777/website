Reverb/Delay Bible

What is reverb?

+

Reverberation is an effect where a sound continues to reverberate after its source has been amplified, due to multiple reflections from room reflections. These reflections interact with each other and gradually fade away, creating a sense of surround sound. In simple terms, it is an "echo" effect, but in a faster, more cohesive form.

Different types of reverbs

+

Room

Simulates reflections in small spaces such as rooms, studios. Provides an intimate, natural sound with relatively short decay times.

Plate

Creates a reverberation effect by simulating the sound of old electromechanical devices in which the sound passed through a metal plate. It is characterized by a bright, dense and slightly metallic tone, with a fast initial dispersion and a smooth decay.

Spring

Simulates the reverb created by the vibration of a spring, often used in guitar amplifiers. Has a distinctive "boingy" character with metallic overtones.

Digital

Created using computer algorithms, simulating various acoustic spaces, or using ready-made samples. Offers the most flexibility and clean sound.

Convolution

A type of digital audio processing that uses impulse responses (IR) to create realistic reverberation sounds by simulating the acoustics of various real-world spaces. Captures the exact sonic characteristics of famous venues and spaces.
